The problem

Standard metrics mix up appearance and meaning.

For EEG-to-image reconstruction, “looks close” is not the same as “means the same thing.” The page now foregrounds that distinction visually before going into method details.

Failure 01

Metric harshness

Low-level metrics punish blur and noise even when the object or scene is still recoverable.

Semantic blindness

A clean-looking image can receive credit while depicting a different object, category, or context.

Our solution

Ask what survived perceptually and what survived semantically.

BCS separates perceptual recoverability from semantic recoverability, then distills multi-VLM consensus into a reusable coherence evaluator.

  1. 01

    Route targeted questions

    Layout, shape, texture, color, category, identity, function, count, and context are evaluated separately.

  2. 02

    Use diverse VLM judges

    Four open-weight models reduce dependence on any single model’s calibration quirks.

  3. 03

    Distill consensus

    A compact BCS head predicts coherence without repeatedly running large VLM evaluators.
